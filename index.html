About

p>

I build engineering organizations
that solve problems other teams can't.

div>

Over 17 years, I've led capital programs totaling $1.5B+ in automated material handling infrastructure, stood up teams from scratch, and created frameworks that became permanent standard work across some of the most complex fulfillment environments in the world.

At Amazon, I lead the Tiger Team within Global Engineering Services — a specialized cross-functional engineering organization that addresses systemic performance gaps in facilities representing $100M–$1B+ in capital investment. We operate as internal consultants and force multipliers, embedded directly into the most challenging facility environments.

I maintain direct OPEX accountability of ~$3.5M in personnel costs alongside my capital portfolio, coordinating across 50+ organizations and influencing stakeholders from Director through VP level. My work consistently produces frameworks, playbooks, and standard work that outlast any single program.

Before Amazon, I led a 45-person team managing half of a station's $75M annual budget at Duke Energy, where I delivered $182M in revenue loss avoidance across parallel capital improvement programs.

I'm now seeking a VP-level platform with full P&L accountability, broader organizational scope, and equity participation — in fulfillment, logistics, industrial automation, or MHE systems integration.
